I research with Prof. Christian Franck to understand the mechanical force signatures of invasive cancer cells. I design experiments and develop algorithms for high-resolution 3D motion tracking and quantitative analysis of large image datasets to measure forces exerted by biological cells.

I am incredibly interested in artificial intelligence, and have built traditional machine learning and deep learning models for real-world applications in classification, computer vision, natural language processing, and recommendation. As a research project in computer science, I developed visualization for 3D stress tensor in VR and performed hypothesis testing for design evaluation.

Outside of work, I travel frequently, enjoy football and click pictures of the Milky Way.

I will defend my Ph.D. degree in early 2019. Currently, I am actively looking for full-time data scientist and/or machine learning engineer positions.
