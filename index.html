I am currently an Engineering - Solid Mechanics Ph.D. Candidate working with Prof. Christian Franck, and I completed masters degree in Computer Science at Brown University.

My Ph.D. research involves designing computational techniques and experiments to understand the role of physical forces on biological cells. My work focuses on developing robust and efficient algorithms for high resolution motion tracking, 3D image analysis, quantitative analyses of large 3D image datasets, and visualizations to explore cell forces. Research studies include investigating mechanical signatures of cancer cells, sperm motility and white blood cells, and modelling 3D cellular force transmission.

In my masters in computer science, I focused on machine learning, computer vision, and scientific visualization. My research involved visualization of 6D tensors in VR. In my personal and course projects, I have built traditional machine learning and deep learning models for applications in classifications, computer vision, natural language processing, and recommendations.

I will be defending my Ph.D. degree in early 2019, and am currently actively looking for full-time data scientist and/or machine learning engineer positions.
